How Does Blockchain Work?

Blockchain Fundamentals: Step-by-Step

  1. Many participants maintain a shared ledger

    Blockchain is a distributed ledger technology where multiple computers (nodes) maintain identical copies of the transaction history. This eliminates the need for a central authority to verify transactions.

  2. Transactions are grouped into blocks

    Multiple transactions are bundled together into a "block." Each block has a unique cryptographic signature (hash) and contains a reference to the previous block, creating a chain of blocks — hence the name "blockchain."

  3. Blocks are cryptographically linked

    Each block contains the cryptographic hash of the previous block, creating an unbroken chain. This makes the blockchain immutable — if someone tries to alter a past transaction, all subsequent blocks would need to be changed, which is computationally impractical.

  4. Consensus ensures trust and security

    Blockchain networks use consensus mechanisms (like Proof of Work or Proof of Stake) to agree on the state of the ledger. This replaces the need for trust in a central authority with trust in the mathematical principles that secure the network.

Benefits of Blockchain for Charity

Complete Transparency

All donations are recorded on the public blockchain, allowing donors to track exactly where their funds go. This creates unprecedented accountability in the charity sector.

Reduced Overhead

Smart contracts automate many processes that traditionally require human intervention, reducing administrative costs and ensuring more of your donation reaches those in need.

Global Accessibility

Blockchain technology enables donations from anywhere in the world, helping charities reach a wider donor base and provide aid to regions that may be underserved by traditional financial systems.

Smart Contracts Explained

At the heart of our platform is a smart contract — self-executing code that lives on the Ethereum blockchain.

Smart contracts are:

  • Autonomous — They execute automatically when conditions are met
  • Transparent — Anyone can verify their code and execution
  • Immutable — Once deployed, they cannot be changed
  • Secure — Cryptographically protected from tampering

Our Badir donation contract handles the entire donation process, from receiving funds to tracking progress toward our goal, all without intermediaries.
